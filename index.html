Background: The ADU Opportunity

What Is an Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU)?

An Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) is a secondary housing unit located on a single-family lot—think backyard cottage, garage apartment, or tiny home. These fully independent living spaces include their own kitchen, bathroom, and sleeping area.



Thanks to recent changes in Austin’s HOME initiative, most single-family lots are now eligible for up to two additional housing units, opening new possibilities for homeowners.

Why Add an ADU to Your Property?

Flexible Living Space

Create room for multigenerational living, a home office, or a private guest retreat—right in your backyard.

Generate Income

Turn your ADU into a rental unit that helps offset your mortgage, build savings, or fund your retirement goals.

Increase Property Value

Boost your home’s value while making smarter use of the land you already own.
